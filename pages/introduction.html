Introduction to Elephant

Elephants are known to be the largest land animal living in this world. Due to their: long noises/trunks,massive floopy ears,and their thick legs. In this planet there are two types of elephant, the asian and the african elephant. Both species have diffrent charecteristics. Why they are important

These animals recive a lot of respect, with whom they share their grounds with. Giving them a strong cultural significance.

During the dry seasons the elephants use their tusks to dig throw mud to get water.Not Only this helps the elephants themselves, butt it also helps the animals sharing the same landscape.

When forest elephants eat, they create gaps in the vegetation. These gaps allow new plants to grow and create pathways for other smaller animals to use. They are also one of the major ways in which trees disperse their seeds; some species rely entirely upon elephants for seed dispersal.

Wherever they live, elephants leave dung that is full of seeds from the many plants they eat. When this dung is deposited the seeds are sown and grow into new grasses, bushes and trees, boosting the health of the savannah ecosystem.
